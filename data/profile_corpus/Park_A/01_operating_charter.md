# Park A — Operating Charter (excerpt, revised 2025-Q2)

**Park designation.** Park A is registered as a renewable-rich office-commercial
park under the regional low-carbon park program. Distributed PV is the primary
on-site asset, with a 2.10× scaled rated capacity relative to the regional
reference template. Energy storage is sized at the reference capacity and is
operated as a renewable-shaping resource rather than as a primary arbitrage
asset.

**Operator authority.** The park operator is authorised to dispatch all
behind-the-meter assets within the operating envelope set by the technical
committee. The operator may unilaterally curtail PV only when distribution
network signals require it; otherwise PV is exported to the grid or used to
charge the local ESS by default. The operator does *not* have authority to
deviate from the published carbon-aware export schedule without prior approval
from the sustainability committee.

**Demand-side coordination.** Internal tenants accept a coordinated comfort
band of ±1.2°C on HVAC setpoints during business hours. Shiftable service
loads (e.g., signage, secondary lighting, water heating) are subject to a
daily energy floor but their hourly distribution is at the operator's
discretion. EV charging is offered to tenants on a best-effort basis with a
guaranteed daily energy quota; deferral within the parking window is
permitted.

**Inter-park interaction.** Park A acts primarily as a supplier in the local
park-to-park market. The charter directs the operator to prioritise bilateral
trades that displace high-carbon grid imports for neighbouring parks. Trade
counterparties are selected with explicit preference for carbon-aware buyers.

**Reporting cadence.** Weekly carbon dashboards are released to all internal
stakeholders; monthly sustainability reviews are submitted to the parent
holding company.
