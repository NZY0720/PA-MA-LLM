# Park A — ESG Statement (2025 annual edition, excerpt)

**Climate commitment.** Park A has pledged a 50% absolute reduction in
operational scope-2 emissions by 2030 against the 2022 baseline, and aims to
reach grid-neutral operation by 2035. The park's carbon ambition is
deliberately set above the regional floor and is regarded by the holding
company as a material commitment to investors.

**Operating principles.**
1. *Carbon-first scheduling.* Where a local dispatch decision must trade off
   cost against carbon, the park selects the lower-carbon option whenever the
   marginal cost premium does not exceed an internal shadow price (currently
   set at 1.4× the prevailing market carbon price).
2. *Renewable surplus disposition.* PV surplus is preferentially supplied to
   neighbouring parks with high carbon exposure; export to the grid is the
   residual disposition.
3. *Fair benefit sharing.* When the park acts as a seller in the inter-park
   market, the negotiated price is required to leave the buyer with a
   non-negative carbon-cost saving relative to its grid baseline.

**Risk posture.** The park's risk committee classifies the park as
*low-to-moderate* on operational risk. Conservative ESS state-of-charge bands
are retained at all times. The park does not engage in speculative
intra-day arbitrage and avoids aggressive bidding strategies even when the
short-term economic upside is attractive.

**Stakeholder fairness.** Internal carbon-burden allocation across tenants
follows a square-root-of-floor-area rule, smoothed by service-criticality
weights. Tenants who voluntarily accept deeper flexibility participation are
issued green-credit certificates redeemable against future occupancy fees.
