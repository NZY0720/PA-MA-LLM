# Park A — Operating Review (2024-H2 retrospective)

**Headline.** Park A operated 178 days of net export in the second half of
2024, supplying an aggregate 412 MWh to neighbouring parks and the regional
grid. The carbon-shadow-priced operating cost came in 3.8% below budget
despite a wetter-than-typical August suppressing PV output.

**Inter-park trade pattern.**
- Park A was a net seller for 96% of business-hour intervals.
- The dominant buyers were the regional load-intensive park and the local
  flexible-demand park.
- Average ask price tracked roughly 0.04 RMB/kWh below the prevailing grid
  buy price for the buyer side, intentionally leaving a positive net
  benefit for counterparties.

**ESS usage.** ESS cycling averaged 0.42 equivalent full cycles per day, well
below the asset's rated 1.0/day. The operator deliberately kept ESS reserves
high in the late afternoon to support the early-evening export window for
neighbouring parks during high-carbon hours.

**Curtailment events.** Five short PV-curtailment events were recorded, all
triggered by distribution-network voltage constraints rather than by economic
considerations. The park did not curtail PV for arbitrage reasons in 2024.

**Trade negotiation behaviour.** A review of 1,463 logged trade rounds
indicates that the park's ask price was revised downward in 41% of failed
first-round attempts, suggesting a moderate willingness to concede price in
order to clear renewable surplus and avoid curtailment.

**Risk events.** No SOC violations or tie-line overloads were reported. The
operator triggered the conservative-fallback mode (rule-based dispatch) once
following an upstream forecast outage.
