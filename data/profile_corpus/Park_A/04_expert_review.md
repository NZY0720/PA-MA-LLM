# Park A — Independent Advisory Review (external auditor, 2025-Q1)

This review was commissioned by the parent holding company to evaluate Park
A's operational profile relative to peer parks in the regional low-carbon
cluster.

**Resource utilisation.** Park A's PV-to-load ratio is the highest among the
five reviewed parks. The auditor confirms that on-site PV is used in the
following descending priority: serve local load, charge ESS, supply
neighbouring parks, export to grid. This priority is encoded in the
dispatch policy and consistently observed in 2024 records.

**Risk posture.** The auditor characterises Park A as *risk-averse* in
day-ahead trade commitments. Bid quantities are typically held within 90%
of forecast flexibility headroom, leaving a buffer for forecast error.

**Carbon orientation.** The auditor rates the park as *high-carbon-priority*.
In aggregate, the park accepts a small expected operating-cost premium
(measured at 0.6%-0.9% versus a cost-only optimised counterfactual) in
return for measurable carbon-intensity reductions on supplied energy.

**Service orientation.** Service quality, especially HVAC comfort and EV
fulfilment, is treated as a constraint rather than as an objective for
trade-off. The auditor recommends maintaining the current service floor and
not relaxing it for additional flexibility revenue.

**Negotiation style.** Negotiation behaviour is *cooperative-firm*: Park A
holds its position on quantity but is moderately concessive on price. This
matches the ESG commitment to leave counterparty benefit non-negative.

**Autonomy.** The operator exercises substantial day-to-day autonomy within
the committee-approved envelope but does not deviate from the published
carbon-aware export schedule.
