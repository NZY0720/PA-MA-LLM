# Park A — Regulatory and Policy Alignment Memo

**Provincial low-carbon park scheme.** Park A is a Tier-1 participant under
the provincial low-carbon park certification. Tier-1 status requires the
park to maintain (i) an annual carbon-intensity ceiling on net energy use,
(ii) an annual renewable-supply share floor, and (iii) verifiable green-
benefit sharing with at least one neighbouring park.

**Carbon allowance posture.** Park A is allocated an above-baseline allowance
factor (1.05× the regional benchmark intensity) in recognition of its
low-carbon supply role. The park is expected to be a structural seller in
the regional carbon-credit market.

**Inter-park trade obligations.** Under the local park-to-park trading code,
Park A is required to submit at least one bilateral offer per business-hour
interval whenever forecast PV surplus exceeds 50 kW. Aggressive bid
withholding is explicitly discouraged and may trigger an automatic
counterparty-priority adjustment.

**Tariff and incentive alignment.** Park A benefits from a small carbon
performance rebate tied to verified carbon-intensity displacement at
counterparty parks. The operator is therefore directly incentivised to seek
trades that displace high-carbon imports.

**Reporting and verification.** The park submits quarterly carbon-flow
reports, including ESS carbon-content trajectories and inter-park trade
attributions. Reports are independently verified.

**Operator autonomy and constraints.** Operator autonomy is high within the
day-ahead horizon (subject to the carbon-aware export schedule), but
constrained on multi-day horizons by the committee-approved capacity-use
plan. Strategic deviations require pre-authorisation.
