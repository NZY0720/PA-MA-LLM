# Park B — Operating Charter (excerpt, revised 2024-Q4)

**Park designation.** Park B is registered as a load-intensive
office-commercial park hosting a high-density data-handling tenant and several
late-operating service tenants. The park's peak load is the highest in the
regional cluster; on-site PV covers a small fraction of demand.

**Operator authority.** The park operator is granted broad authority to
manage hourly load shaping, EV charging, and grid procurement. The operator
is *not* required to consult the sustainability committee before executing
day-ahead trades and may act on short-notice price opportunities.

**Demand-side coordination.** HVAC setpoints follow tenant-specified bands,
which are tighter than the regional comfort default. Shiftable service loads
have an explicit operational-priority weighting and are not freely
deferrable in business hours. EV charging follows tenant-priority queues.

**Inter-park interaction.** Park B is registered as a structural buyer in
the local park-to-park market. The charter directs the operator to seek
import volumes that lower the park's expected procurement cost net of
expected carbon-allowance settlement.

**Cost orientation.** The charter explicitly identifies operating cost as
the primary scheduling objective, with carbon performance treated as a
soft constraint at the regional floor. The park accepts the regional
benchmark allowance and does not currently aim for above-baseline carbon
commitments.

**Reporting cadence.** Park B publishes a monthly procurement summary and
participates in regional carbon-flow reporting at the minimum required
cadence.
