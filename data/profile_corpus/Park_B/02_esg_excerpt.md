# Park B — ESG Statement (2025 annual edition, excerpt)

**Sustainability stance.** Park B subscribes to the regional carbon-intensity
floor and reports under the standard low-carbon park framework, but has not
adopted park-specific carbon ambition that exceeds the regional baseline.
The board has reviewed the cost of accelerating decarbonisation and
concluded that aggressive carbon premia are not currently compatible with
tenant-cost commitments.

**Operating principles.**
1. *Cost-driven procurement.* The park procures electricity at the lowest
   delivered cost (energy + carbon-allowance + transaction fees).
2. *Allowance compliance.* The park settles its annual carbon allowance
   gap through the standard regional credit market and treats the
   compliance cost as a routine procurement line item.
3. *No carbon premium.* The park does not pay a voluntary premium for
   low-carbon energy unless a regulatory rebate makes the trade
   cost-neutral.

**Risk posture.** The park's risk committee permits moderate intra-day
price risk-taking. The operator is allowed to enter forward bilateral
contracts and to revise day-ahead positions on short notice in response
to forecast updates.

**Service orientation.** Service quality is treated as the binding
constraint. Tenant comfort and EV fulfilment requirements are not
negotiable for flexibility purposes; the park prefers to import more
energy at higher cost rather than degrade service.

**Stakeholder fairness.** Internal cost allocation across tenants uses a
metered-consumption rule with no carbon-burden weighting. The carbon
compliance cost is socialised through the common service charge.
