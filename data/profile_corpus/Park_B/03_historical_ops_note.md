# Park B — Operating Review (2024-H2 retrospective)

**Headline.** Park B procured 6.81 GWh from the grid and 0.42 GWh from
inter-park imports in the second half of 2024. Inter-park imports
displaced an estimated 96 tonnes of CO2 versus a grid-only baseline.
Total realised procurement cost came in 1.1% above the cost-only
forecast, primarily due to two evening peak-pricing events.

**Inter-park trade pattern.**
- Park B was a structural buyer in 91% of business-hour intervals when an
  offer was available.
- The dominant counterparty was Park A; secondary counterparties were
  Parks C and E.
- Average bid price was approximately 0.02 RMB/kWh above the prevailing
  grid sell price, leaving a positive margin to the seller; the operator
  did *not* attempt to push bids below the grid-sell floor.

**ESS usage.** ESS cycling averaged 0.65 equivalent full cycles per day
and was used primarily to manage evening-peak demand charges. ESS was
not used as an arbitrage asset against carbon signals.

**Trade negotiation behaviour.** Out of 1,287 logged trade rounds, the
operator raised its bid in 38% of failed first-round attempts and held
position in 54%, indicating a moderately concessive bidding style
focused on closing high-value imports rather than maximising counterparty
choice.

**Risk events.** Two short tie-line near-limit events were recorded
during compound peak-load and peak-EV hours; both were resolved by
routing additional imports through inter-park trade rather than by
shedding load.
