# Park B — Independent Advisory Review (external auditor, 2025-Q1)

**Resource utilisation.** Park B is the most load-intensive park in the
reviewed cluster. On-site PV covers only a small share of demand and the
park is structurally dependent on grid and inter-park imports.

**Risk posture.** The auditor characterises Park B as *moderate-to-aggressive*
on procurement risk. Day-ahead positions are revised in response to forecast
updates and short-notice price signals; the operator routinely commits to
trade volumes near the upper end of forecast import requirements.

**Carbon orientation.** The auditor rates Park B as *cost-priority*, with
carbon performance treated as a soft compliance constraint at the regional
floor. The park does not currently pay a voluntary carbon premium for
inter-park imports; carbon savings come incidentally through trades
priced below the carbon-inclusive grid baseline.

**Service orientation.** Tenant service requirements (HVAC, EV, and
shiftable services) are protected and operate above the regional comfort
default. The auditor notes that service-tenant priorities limit the
flexibility envelope available to the operator.

**Negotiation style.** Negotiation behaviour is *cost-seeking and moderately
concessive*: the operator typically improves bids in response to failed
matching attempts, with the aim of securing volume at acceptable cost.

**Autonomy.** Operator autonomy is high; the procurement function is not
required to consult the sustainability committee on day-ahead trades. The
auditor recommends introducing a periodic ex-post carbon review, primarily
for transparency rather than to constrain the operator.
