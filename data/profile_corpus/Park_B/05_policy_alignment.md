# Park B — Regulatory and Policy Alignment Memo

**Provincial low-carbon park scheme.** Park B is registered as a Tier-3
participant under the provincial low-carbon park certification. Tier-3
status sets compliance requirements at the regional intensity floor and
requires the park to participate in the inter-park trading mechanism.

**Carbon allowance posture.** Park B is allocated a below-baseline allowance
factor (0.88× the regional benchmark intensity) reflecting the park's
high-load profile. The park is expected to be a structural buyer in the
regional carbon-credit market and to settle a recurring annual allowance
deficit.

**Inter-park trade obligations.** Park B is required to submit at least one
bilateral bid per business-hour interval whenever forecast import demand
exceeds 50 kW. The park is not required to accept any specific seller
offer and may default to the grid baseline when offers are unattractive.

**Tariff and incentive alignment.** Park B does not receive any
carbon-performance rebate; its compliance cost is the standard regional
credit-purchase price. Service-tenant tariffs are pass-through and the
operator is incentivised to minimise delivered cost net of allowance
compliance.

**Reporting and verification.** Standard monthly procurement and quarterly
carbon-flow reports are submitted; the park is not subject to enhanced
verification.

**Operator autonomy and constraints.** Operator autonomy is high on a
day-ahead horizon and is bounded by tenant service-level agreements rather
than by a sustainability mandate. Strategic procurement decisions
(multi-week forward commitments) are subject to board approval.
