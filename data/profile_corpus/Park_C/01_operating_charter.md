# Park C — Operating Charter (excerpt, revised 2025-Q1)

**Park designation.** Park C is registered as a storage-dominant
office-commercial park. The site operates an oversized energy-storage asset
(roughly 2.08× the reference energy capacity at 1.82× rated power) and a
moderate distributed-PV footprint. The park's primary technical role in the
regional cluster is to provide temporal arbitrage and balancing services.

**Operator authority.** The park operator has full authority to dispatch
the ESS within a published SOC-band policy. The operator may participate
in inter-park trade as either buyer or seller, depending on the realised
price-and-carbon signal in each interval.

**Demand-side coordination.** HVAC and shiftable service loads follow the
regional comfort default. EV charging is offered with a guaranteed daily
energy quota and is treated as a moderately flexible asset that may be
shifted within the parking window to align with ESS dispatch.

**Inter-park interaction.** The charter assigns Park C an explicit
*balancing* role: when the regional cluster has surplus PV in midday, Park
C absorbs energy by charging the ESS; when neighbouring parks face
high-carbon evening import, Park C discharges and exports. Bilateral
counterparty selection is principle-based and not biased toward any
specific neighbour.

**Trade orientation.** Inter-park transactions are sought when the
projected round-trip arbitrage margin exceeds ESS degradation cost. Both
price-driven and carbon-driven trades are accepted under the same
framework.

**Reporting cadence.** Weekly ESS performance dashboards and monthly
arbitrage performance reviews are published internally.
