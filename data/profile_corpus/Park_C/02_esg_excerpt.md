# Park C — ESG Statement (2025 annual edition, excerpt)

**Sustainability stance.** Park C commits to maintain carbon intensity at
or modestly below the regional baseline. Its principal contribution to the
regional decarbonisation agenda is *temporal carbon shifting* through
storage operation, rather than direct emission reduction through on-site
generation.

**Operating principles.**
1. *Carbon-aware arbitrage.* When charging the ESS, the operator
   prefers low-carbon hours; when discharging, the operator prefers
   high-carbon hours, subject to round-trip economic feasibility.
2. *Balancing service.* The park provides paid balancing services to
   neighbouring parks and accepts both directions of bilateral trade.
3. *Moderate carbon premium.* The park is willing to accept a small
   carbon-driven cost premium (capped at 0.4% of operating cost) on ESS
   dispatch when the carbon displacement is materially large.

**Risk posture.** The risk committee classifies Park C as *moderate* on
operational risk. The ESS is operated within a conservative SOC band of
[15%, 90%] to preserve cycle life. Aggressive arbitrage strategies that
push the SOC band are explicitly disallowed.

**Service orientation.** Tenant service is treated as a baseline
constraint; the park does not over-deliver service beyond the regional
default.

**Stakeholder fairness.** Internal carbon-burden allocation uses a
load-weighted rule, smoothed by an ESS-discharge attribution adjustment
when stored low-carbon energy is delivered to a tenant.
