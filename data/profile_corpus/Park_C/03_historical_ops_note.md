# Park C — Operating Review (2024-H2 retrospective)

**Headline.** Park C executed 184 days of active arbitrage with an
average of 1.6 equivalent full ESS cycles per day. The park acted as a
seller in 53% of business-hour intervals when it transacted, and as a
buyer in 47%. Realised arbitrage margin covered ESS degradation cost by
1.7×.

**Inter-park trade pattern.**
- Counterparties were broadly distributed across the four other parks;
  no single counterparty exceeded 35% of trade volume.
- The park frequently switched roles between adjacent hours, consistent
  with its balancing mandate.
- Average ask-bid spread on the park's side was approximately 0.03
  RMB/kWh, smaller than the cluster average, reflecting a pragmatic
  willingness to clear at near-market prices.

**ESS usage.** The ESS spent 38% of the period in charging mode, 42% in
discharging mode, and 20% in standby. Charging-time carbon intensity
averaged 0.49 kg/kWh; discharging-time average displacement intensity
was 0.59 kg/kWh, indicating a positive carbon shift on the order of 0.10
kg/kWh per delivered kWh.

**Trade negotiation behaviour.** Across 1,512 logged trade rounds, the
operator concluded most trades within two rounds. Bid-ask revisions were
moderate (typical revision step 0.01-0.02 RMB/kWh) and balanced between
price improvement and quantity adjustment.

**Risk events.** No SOC violations were recorded. The park hit the
upper SOC band twice during extended midday surplus, which was managed
by curtailing ESS charging rather than by exporting at a loss.
