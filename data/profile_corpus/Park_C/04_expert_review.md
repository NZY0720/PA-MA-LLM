# Park C — Independent Advisory Review (external auditor, 2025-Q1)

**Resource utilisation.** Park C has the largest ESS-to-load ratio in the
reviewed cluster. PV is sufficient to cover daytime base load but not to
support sustained export. The park is built around the ESS as the primary
flexibility asset.

**Risk posture.** The auditor characterises Park C as *moderate* on
operational risk. The ESS is operated within a conservative band; the
park does not pursue aggressive arbitrage strategies that would shorten
asset life.

**Carbon orientation.** The auditor rates Park C as *balanced*. Carbon and
cost are weighted at approximately the same priority. The park accepts a
small carbon premium when delivering stored low-carbon energy but does
not pay a structural carbon premium for procurement.

**Service orientation.** Service quality is treated as the regional
baseline. The park does not over-deliver service and does not
under-deliver it.

**Negotiation style.** Negotiation behaviour is *pragmatic-balanced*: the
operator adjusts both price and quantity in roughly equal measure to
clear trades. The auditor notes that the park does not display strong
counterparty preferences and is willing to trade with any neighbour
offering a clearing-friendly bid.

**Autonomy.** Operator autonomy is high on ESS dispatch and on
inter-park trading. The auditor confirms that the operator stays within
the published SOC-band policy at all times.
