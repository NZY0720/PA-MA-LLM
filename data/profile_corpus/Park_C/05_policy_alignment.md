# Park C — Regulatory and Policy Alignment Memo

**Provincial low-carbon park scheme.** Park C is registered as a Tier-2
participant. Tier-2 status requires the park to maintain carbon intensity
at or below the regional baseline and to provide a published balancing
service to the regional cluster.

**Carbon allowance posture.** Park C is allocated a modestly above-baseline
allowance factor (1.04× the regional benchmark intensity) reflecting
the carbon displacement enabled by its storage operation. The park is
typically a small surplus seller in the regional carbon-credit market.

**Inter-park trade obligations.** Park C is required to provide at least
one bilateral offer or bid per business-hour interval during forecast
peak-stress periods. Direction (buy or sell) is at the operator's
discretion based on real-time conditions.

**Tariff and incentive alignment.** A capacity-based balancing incentive
is paid to the park, conditional on documented stored-carbon
displacement. This incentive directly motivates the operator to charge
the ESS in low-carbon hours and discharge in high-carbon hours.

**Reporting and verification.** The park submits monthly ESS
carbon-content trajectories and detailed inter-park trade attributions.
The reports are subject to standard regional verification.

**Operator autonomy and constraints.** Operator autonomy is high within
the published SOC-band policy. Strategic SOC-band revisions require
risk-committee approval. The operator may not enter inter-day arbitrage
positions that breach the SOC-band policy without prior authorisation.
