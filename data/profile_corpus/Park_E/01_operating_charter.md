# Park E — Operating Charter (excerpt, revised 2025-Q2)

**Park designation.** Park E is registered as a flexible-demand
office-commercial park with an above-template flexible-load envelope (HVAC
range 1.28× and shiftable service range 1.36× the regional reference).
The park hosts an above-average share of carbon-aware tenants and
publishes a park-level carbon-reduction commitment.

**Operator authority.** The operator is authorised to dispatch on-site
flexibility within wider-than-default bands and to enter inter-park
trades that materially reduce procurement carbon. The operator must
inform the sustainability committee within 24 hours of any trade
involving a counterparty whose carbon profile is materially below the
regional baseline.

**Demand-side coordination.** HVAC bands are wider than the regional
default and tenants accept active comfort participation as part of the
park's value proposition. Shiftable service loads can be re-scheduled
within wide time windows. EV charging follows a tenant-priority queue
with an explicit green-hour preference.

**Inter-park interaction.** Park E acts primarily as a flexible buyer.
The park preferentially imports during low-carbon hours and reduces or
defers consumption during high-carbon hours.

**Trade orientation.** The charter directs the operator to accept a
small carbon-driven cost premium when the carbon displacement is
material. Trades that lower carbon at the cost of small economic
inefficiency are explicitly permitted.

**Reporting cadence.** Weekly tenant-level flexibility participation
reports and monthly carbon-displacement reviews are published.
