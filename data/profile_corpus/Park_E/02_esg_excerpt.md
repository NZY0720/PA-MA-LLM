# Park E — ESG Statement (2025 annual edition, excerpt)

**Sustainability stance.** Park E has adopted an above-baseline carbon
ambition tied to its flexible-demand tenant mix. The park targets a 35%
reduction in operational carbon intensity by 2028 against the 2022
baseline and a published tenant-shared green-hour participation rate of
70% by 2027.

**Operating principles.**
1. *Carbon-aware demand shifting.* The park preferentially shifts flexible
   load into low-carbon hours and accepts a small comfort or convenience
   cost when the carbon displacement is material.
2. *Selective procurement.* The park preferentially imports from
   counterparties whose carbon profile is below the regional baseline.
3. *Tenant participation.* The park operates a green-credit programme
   that rewards tenants for accepting deeper flexibility participation.

**Risk posture.** The risk committee classifies Park E as
*moderate-conservative* on operational risk. The park is willing to
accept some forecast and trade-execution risk to capture carbon
displacement, but does not engage in speculative arbitrage strategies.

**Service orientation.** Service quality is treated as a participatory
contract with tenants: tenants accept wider comfort bands in exchange
for green-credit rewards. The park does not relax service unilaterally
beyond the agreed bands.

**Stakeholder fairness.** Internal carbon-burden allocation uses an
*opt-in* rule: tenants who join the green-hour programme receive
favourable carbon attribution; others receive standard metered
attribution.
