# Park E — Operating Review (2024-H2 retrospective)

**Headline.** Park E shifted 1.18 GWh of flexible demand into
below-median-carbon hours in 2024-H2, equivalent to an avoided 142
tonnes of CO2 against an inflexible-baseline counterfactual. Realised
operating cost came in 1.6% above the cost-only counterfactual; tenant
green-credit payouts were 0.7% of operating cost.

**Inter-park trade pattern.**
- Park E acted as a buyer in 71% of business-hour intervals in which it
  transacted and as a seller in 29% (driven by mid-day flexible-load
  curtailment that occasionally produced small export pockets).
- The dominant counterparty was Park A; secondary counterparties were
  Parks C and D.
- Average bid price was approximately 0.025 RMB/kWh above the grid sell
  baseline, with an explicit upward bid bias during high-carbon hours.

**Flexible-load usage.** HVAC shifted 480 MWh and shiftable service
loads shifted 230 MWh into low-carbon hours. EV charging was deferred
to low-carbon evening windows in 64% of charging sessions.

**Trade negotiation behaviour.** Across 1,318 logged trade rounds, the
operator was moderately patient: 28% of failed first-round attempts
were retried in the next round without bid revision, indicating
willingness to wait for a lower-carbon counterparty rather than
chasing the first available offer.

**Risk events.** No SOC, tie-line, or service-band violations were
recorded. One internal tenant escalation was resolved by
adjusting the affected tenant's flexibility band.
