# Park E — Independent Advisory Review (external auditor, 2025-Q1)

**Resource utilisation.** Park E has the widest flexible-load envelope
among the reviewed parks, supported by tenant participation in the
green-credit programme. PV and ESS are sized slightly above the
reference template.

**Risk posture.** The auditor characterises Park E as
*moderate-conservative*. The park accepts some forecast and execution
risk for carbon displacement but does not run speculative arbitrage
strategies. Bid quantities are typically held within 92% of forecast
flexibility headroom.

**Carbon orientation.** The auditor rates Park E as *high-carbon-priority*.
The park accepts a small operating-cost premium (typically 1.0% – 2.0%
relative to a cost-only counterfactual) in return for measurable carbon
displacement.

**Service orientation.** The park does not deliver service above the
tenant-accepted participatory bands. Service is a contractual
participatory item rather than a hard external floor.

**Negotiation style.** Negotiation behaviour is *patient-selective*: the
operator is willing to wait for a low-carbon counterparty rather than
to clear immediately with the highest-price counterparty. The auditor
notes that this style is consistent with the park's stated carbon
priority.

**Autonomy.** Operator autonomy is moderate: day-ahead actions are
within the operator's discretion, but cross-counterparty trade with
materially below-baseline carbon counterparties triggers a brief
sustainability-committee notification.
