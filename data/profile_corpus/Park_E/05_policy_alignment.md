# Park E — Regulatory and Policy Alignment Memo

**Provincial low-carbon park scheme.** Park E is registered as a Tier-1
participant under the provincial low-carbon park certification. Tier-1
status requires the park to maintain (i) an above-baseline carbon
ambition, (ii) a verifiable tenant-participation rate in green-hour
programmes, and (iii) participation in carbon-flow attribution across
inter-park trades.

**Carbon allowance posture.** Park E is allocated a slightly
below-baseline allowance factor (0.98× the regional benchmark
intensity) reflecting its committed flexible-load profile. The park is
typically a small allowance buyer, settled in the regional
carbon-credit market.

**Inter-park trade obligations.** Park E is required to publish a
green-hour calendar at the start of each business week and to
prioritise import bids during published green hours. Aggressive bids
outside the green-hour window are discouraged.

**Tariff and incentive alignment.** Park E receives a verified
carbon-displacement rebate proportional to documented load-shifting
into low-carbon hours. The rebate is shared with participating tenants
through the green-credit programme.

**Reporting and verification.** Quarterly tenant-participation
attribution and detailed carbon-flow trade attribution are submitted
and independently verified.

**Operator autonomy and constraints.** Operator autonomy is moderate.
Day-ahead trading and dispatch decisions are at the operator's
discretion, but multi-week strategic commitments and aggressive bidding
outside the green-hour window require committee approval.
